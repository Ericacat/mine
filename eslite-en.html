Eslite Online
EC Site Redesign

I redesigned the online store of Taiwan's famous bookstore brand, Eslite.
The store changed from selling books only to a full lifestyle shop. I worked on the design direction, mobile layout, and the buying steps.

EC Site Redesign

Eslite rebranded from an online bookstore to a full lifestyle destination at the end of 2020. I redesigned the site to reflect that shift — making every product feel just as natural to discover and buy.

Challenge

Challenges

From a Bookstore to a Lifestyle Shop

Eslite is well known as a bookstore. But their physical stores also sell lifestyle goods and gifts. The challenge was to show these products well online, just like books.

Specific Problems

  • A layout designed for books left lifestyle products feeling out of place.
  • Mobile users encountered a design that wasn't built with them in mind.
  • The checkout was too long — many users left before completing their purchase.
  • In-store and online memberships lived in separate worlds.
Solution

Design Approach

1. Wide Layout for the Homepage

I used a wide 1280px layout and changed the banner positions. Users could quickly find featured products. Lifestyle goods and gifts looked just as good as books.

Design Points

  • Wide layout that gives products room to breathe
  • Banners positioned so the right product finds the right user
  • Clear paths for different intentions — browsing, gifting, or buying

2. Responsive Web Design

I worked with the frontend team to use responsive web design — so the experience feels natural on every screen size.

Results

  • The experience feels natural on every screen size
  • One codebase for all devices
  • Better search ranking with SEO

3. One Product Page for Both Books and Goods

I made one product page layout that works for both books and goods. I added more product information to help users decide.

Design Points

  • One flexible layout that lets each product category shine
  • Enough detail to understand what you're buying — and why it matters
  • Related suggestions that feel like natural next steps

4. Shorter Buying Steps

Designed with the user's momentum in mind — 3 clear steps to complete a purchase, with no unnecessary friction along the way.

Design Points

  • 3 steps that feel complete, not truncated
  • Each step asks only what's needed in that moment
  • Users always know where they are and what comes next

5. App Design: In-Store and Online Together

The app redesign made online shopping better. It also kept the in-store member features. Users could use the same account in stores and online.

Results

  • One membership for in-store and online
  • Use points and coupons in both places
  • A connected online and offline experience
Results & Impact

Results & Learnings

Project Results

  • The site became a place where any product — not just books — felt at home
  • Mobile users can browse and buy with the same ease as on desktop
  • A more natural checkout led to fewer users dropping off mid-purchase
  • In-store and online now feel like the same Eslite

What I Learned

  • Led a large-scale EC redesign across product categories and platforms
  • Collaborated closely with the frontend team to bring the responsive experience to life
  • How designing for how people feel — not just what they do — affects whether they complete a purchase
  • How design bridges physical and digital spaces into one coherent brand experience

Reflection

Going from a bookstore to a full lifestyle shop was a big challenge. The best result was building a design that kept Eslite's brand identity while serving many more user needs.
