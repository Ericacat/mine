Challenge

Challenges

Keeping the Design Consistent Across Many Teams

TaipeiPASS connects many different services in one place. Many outside companies helped build it. Each company had their own designers. Without shared design rules, the platform looked and felt different in each section.

Specific Problems

  • Each vendor designed independently, with no shared reference
  • UI components multiplied without structure
  • Users encountered different patterns in different parts of the platform
  • With each new feature, maintaining a cohesive feel became harder
Solution

Updating and Using the Design Guidelines

How the Design Guidelines Changed

At first, there was a Design Guideline. It covered colors, font sizes, and spacing.

As the service grew, we updated the guidelines. We made "user behavior" the main idea. This helped us design based on what users actually do. The result was a more consistent experience across the platform.

My Role in the Guidelines

  • Worked with the UX team to reframe the guidelines around how users actually behave
  • Guided outside vendors to keep the experience consistent throughout the platform

Using the Guidelines in Real Projects

I used these guidelines to design several new features.

Case Studies

Key Projects

1. Touch Feature Website

I designed a website to show how the touch feature works. Users could understand it quickly at a glance.

My Work

  • Designed visuals and flows to communicate the feature at a glance
  • A user flow that feels intuitive to navigate
  • Post-implementation review to ensure the experience held

2. App Feature Redesign

I looked at the current screens and made them easier and better to use.

Key Improvements

  • Information organized so users always know where to look
  • Navigation that guides rather than confuses
  • More inclusive design for a wider range of users
  • Verified the experience after implementation, not just before

3. COVID-19 Information Website

This was a website for COVID-19. Users could check their symptoms and get medical advice. I designed the self-check flow and information pages to be clear and easy to read.

Design Approach

  • Three color zones that make the right information land first
  • A layout calm enough to follow even under stress
  • Designed and delivered quickly for an urgent public need
Results & Impact

Results & Learnings

Project Results

  • Design guidelines gave everyone a shared language — and the platform felt like one
  • Consistency was maintained even across teams that had never worked together before
  • Post-implementation review ensured the intended experience made it to users

What I Learned

  • How to build design guidelines that scale across a large, multi-team platform
  • How to align many stakeholders around a shared experience vision in a government context
  • Presented designs — from initial screens to final proposals — to senior leadership
  • When a team shares ownership of the experience, the result shows it

Reflection

Working on a platform this scale showed me how easily consistency erodes — and how much users notice when it does. Clear guidelines and a disciplined review process were what kept the experience from fragmenting.

Our PM was excellent, and the team worked very well together. It was a great experience to work as one strong team.
