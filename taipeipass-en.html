Challenge

Challenges

Keeping the Design Consistent Across Many Teams

TaipeiPASS connects many different services in one place. Many outside companies helped build it. Each company had their own designers. Without shared design rules, the platform looked and felt different in each section.

Specific Problems

  • Each company made their own design
  • Too many different UI parts were created
  • The platform did not look or feel consistent
  • It was hard to check quality when adding new features
Solution

Updating and Using the Design Guidelines

How the Design Guidelines Changed

At first, there was a Design Guideline. It covered colors, font sizes, and spacing.

As the service grew, we updated the guidelines. We made "user behavior" the main idea. This helped us design based on what users actually do. The result was a more consistent experience across the platform.

My Role in the Guidelines

  • Worked with the UX team to rebuild the guidelines around user behavior
  • Gave design guidance and quality checks to outside vendors

Using the Guidelines in Real Projects

I used these guidelines to design several new features.

This was a website for COVID-19. Users could check their symptoms and get medical advice. I designed the self-check flow and information pages to be clear and easy to read.

Results & Impact

Results & Learnings

Project Results

  • The design guidelines helped keep the platform consistent
  • The design stayed the same even with many different companies involved
  • Design QA helped improve the quality of what was built

What I Learned

  • I learned how to build and use design guidelines for a large platform
  • I learned how to work with many different people in a government project
  • As the senior designer, I presented screen designs to managers and leaders
  • Good teamwork makes the quality of design much better

Reflection

Working on a large platform taught me how hard it is to keep the design consistent. The design guidelines and QA process were the most important tools for this.

Our PM was excellent, and the team worked very well together. It was a great experience to work as one strong team.
